Does the use of renewable energy affect CO2 emissions per capita?

Find out how the use of energy, CO2 emission and generation of waste are connected in European countries. Every line and every dot represent a country. Only interested in countries that use particularly use little energy per capita? Select a low range on the energy-ax in the left graph and find out how those countries score on the other variables.

In the scatterplot underneath emphasis is put on the use of renewable energy. A trend becomes visible: the higher the use of renewable energy per capita, the lower the CO2 emissions per capita. Hover over the circles and the corresponding line in the graph left becomes highlighted. Some countries are only visible in the linegraph due to missing data.


Slide through the years and watch for example Luxembourg's and Iceland's energy use skyrocketing.

The link between Renewable Energy and Emission

Here some text about a country (or more) for which the data shows interesting country. For example: Norway produces all of the energy it uses. How does that connect to the emissions and waste generation of Norway? A Sankey Diagram could be shown on the right, to follow the flow of different energy-sources to pollution.
